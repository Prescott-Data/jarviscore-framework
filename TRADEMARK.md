# JarvisCore Trademark Policy

This document describes the trademark policy for the JarvisCore project.

## Overview

The source code for JarvisCore is open source, licensed under the Apache License 2.0. However, **the JarvisCore name and any official JarvisCore logos are trademarks of Prescott Data** and are not covered by the Apache 2.0 licence.

This policy explains how you may and may not use the JarvisCore name and logo. The purpose of this policy is to protect the integrity of the project and the trust users place in it — not to restrict legitimate open-source activity.

---

## The JarvisCore Name

### Permitted uses

You may use the name "JarvisCore" without written permission when:

- Referring to the JarvisCore open-source project in factual, non-commercial descriptions (e.g., blog posts, talks, documentation, academic papers).
- Saying your software is built on, integrates with, or is compatible with JarvisCore (e.g., "built with JarvisCore", "a JarvisCore plugin").
- Describing your own experience using JarvisCore in community forums, social media, or events.

### Prohibited uses

You may **not** use the name "JarvisCore" without written permission from Prescott Data when:

- Naming a product, service, or company in a way that could imply official affiliation or endorsement (e.g., "JarvisCore Pro", "JarvisCore Cloud" without authorisation).
- Using the name in a domain name, app store listing, or commercial offering in a way that could mislead users into thinking it is an official Prescott Data product.
- Implying that Prescott Data sponsors, endorses, or is affiliated with your project or organisation.
- Modifying the name in a way likely to cause confusion (e.g., "JarvisCor3", "Jarvis-Core").

---

## The JarvisCore Logo

- Only the official JarvisCore logo, as provided by Prescott Data, may be used to represent the project.
- Do not modify, distort, recolour, or alter the logo in any way.
- Do not combine the logo with other logos, imagery, or text in ways that could create confusion about the source or ownership of the mark.
- To obtain the official logo file, contact **info@prescottdata.io**.

---

## Forks and Derivative Works

The Apache 2.0 licence permits you to fork JarvisCore and build derivative works. However:

- A fork must be clearly distinguished from the official JarvisCore project.
- A fork must not use the JarvisCore name or logo as its primary identity without written permission.
- Forks should make clear they are community forks and not official Prescott Data releases.

---

## Community Projects

We welcome and encourage community projects (plugins, integrations, tutorials, examples). You may use the JarvisCore name in the name of a community project where it is clear that the project is community-maintained and not an official Prescott Data product. Example of acceptable naming: "jarviscore-redis-adapter" (a community adapter). Example of unacceptable naming: "JarvisCore Enterprise Community Edition" (implies official product).
